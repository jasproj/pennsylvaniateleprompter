# Professional Teleprompter Services for Trade Shows & Conventions

## Introduction

A major trade convention brings 500-2,000 industry professionals together for 2-3 days. Your keynote speaker is opening the event. Your panel moderator is introducing sessions. Your company executives are delivering updates. Each speaker needs to look confident, prepared, and professional.

Professional teleprompter services are standard infrastructure at conventions and trade shows. This guide covers how trade show and convention teleprompter services work.

---

## Why Trade Shows Use Professional Teleprompters

**Multiple speakers in sequence.** Conventions have 10-50+ speakers over multiple days. Each speaker needs support.

**Large audiences.** Convention sessions often feature 300-1,000+ attendees in a single ballroom.

**Professional expectations.** Industry conferences demand polished, confident speakers.

**Tight schedules.** Speakers must hit exact timing (50-minute keynote, 30-minute breakout session). Teleprompters ensure speakers don't run over.

**No memorization possibility.** Multi-day conventions with multiple speakers—no one can memorize everything.

## Trade Show and Convention Teleprompter System

### Equipment Setup

**Standard Configuration:**
- Dual professional monitors (19-24")
- Positioned on stage left/right
- Visible to audience (expected at conventions)
- Single experienced operator managing all displays
- Backup monitor system

**Alternative:**
- Glass teleprompter (for formal opening ceremonies)
- One system, multiple speakers (adjusted between speakers)

### How Multiple Speakers Work

**Speaker 1 (10:00 AM, 50-minute keynote):**
1. Operator loads speaker's script
2. Speaker takes stage
3. Operator scrolls script at speaker's pace
4. Speaker delivers; audience sees professional presentation

**15-minute break for setup**

**Speakers 2 & 3 (10:50 AM Breakout Session, 25 minutes each):**
1. Between speakers, operator loads next script (30-60 seconds)
2. Speaker 2 delivers
3. Operator switches script, Speaker 3 delivers
4. Seamless transitions

**Repeat throughout day:** 6-8 speakers per day, each with fresh script

### Operator Role at Conventions

Convention teleprompter operators are specialists:

✅ **Script management:** Organize, load, and manage 20-50+ scripts for multiple speakers

✅ **Timing:** Help speakers stay within allocated time slots (crucial for convention schedules)

✅ **Speaker coaching:** Quick tips between speakers on pace, delivery, energy

✅ **Troubleshooting:** Manage technical issues (display brightness, script formatting issues)

✅ **Coordination:** Work with convention organizers, AV team, program coordinators

✅ **Flexibility:** Handle last-minute speaker changes, script updates, timing adjustments

## Trade Show vs. Convention Differences

**Trade Show:**
- More informal
- Smaller audience per presentation
- Shorter sessions (15-30 minutes)
- Less rehearsal time
- More about product pitches than messaging

**Convention:**
- Formal structure
- Large audiences
- Longer sessions (45-90 minutes for keynotes)
- More rehearsal expected
- Professional messaging critical

**Teleprompter approach:** Similar but conventions typically involve more speakers and more coordination.

  - Panel discussion (may need flexibility for Q&A)
  - Closing remarks from organizers

**Result:**

## Convention Teleprompter Best Practices

### For Convention Organizers

✅ **Collect scripts 1 week before convention**
- Gives operator time to format and organize
- Allows for any technical issues

✅ **Brief speakers on teleprompter use**
- Not all speakers have used professional teleprompters
- Quick orientation meeting saves time

✅ **Coordinate with AV team**
- Teleprompter operator and convention AV team must communicate
- Lighting, camera angles, display positioning all coordinated

✅ **Plan for speaker changeovers**
- 5-10 minutes between speakers allows operator to load next script
- Quick rehearsal between different speaker styles

✅ **Prepare backup speakers**
- If a speaker cancels, have a backup speaker ready with teleprompter support

### For Convention Speakers

✅ **Provide script 48+ hours before**
- Operator can format and test
- You can rehearse if desired

✅ **Test teleprompter during rehearsal**
- Many speakers are new to professional teleprompters
- Quick test run removes anxiety

✅ **Communicate pacing preferences**
- Do you speak fast or slow?
- Any emphasis points in script?
- Operator will adjust to you

✅ **Understand you can ad-lib**
- If you want to improvise, you can
- Operator will follow and return to script

✅ **Arrive early for session**
- Quick 5-minute tech check before you go live
- Final display position adjustment if needed

## Convention Teleprompter FAQ

**Q: What if a speaker hasn't rehearsed with the teleprompter?**
A: Operator can do quick 5-10 minute orientation right before speech. Most speakers get comfortable very quickly.

**Q: Can you handle last-minute speaker changes?**
A: Yes, if you provide the new speaker's script within 1-2 hours. Operator can quickly format and load new script.

**Q: What if sessions run late and disrupt the schedule?**
A: Operator manages this by adjusting scripts or removing non-critical sections to keep timing tight.

**Q: Can multiple speakers share one teleprompter display?**
A: Yes, but there's a 1-2 minute changeover between speakers (operator resets display, adjusts brightness for next speaker).

**Q: How do you handle panel discussions?**
A: Panel discussions are trickier (interactive, less structured). Operator can provide talking points on teleprompter if needed, but panelists usually deliver more naturally without teleprompter.

**Q: What if the speaker wants to deviate from the script?**
A: No problem. Operator follows the ad-lib and returns to script seamlessly.
