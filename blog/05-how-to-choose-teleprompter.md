# How to Choose a Teleprompter for Your Event: Complete Decision Guide

## Introduction

You've got an important event coming up. You know a teleprompter would help your speaker deliver with confidence. But there are so many options—glass systems, professional monitors, tablets, DIY solutions—and you're not sure which one is right for your situation.

This guide walks you through the exact decision process to choose the right teleprompter system for your specific event, budget, and needs.

**Spoiler:** The "best" teleprompter isn't the most expensive one. It's the one that fits YOUR event requirements, venue, and budget.

---

## The Teleprompter Decision Matrix

Before diving into equipment types, ask yourself these 5 questions. Your answers determine which system is right for you.

### Question 1: How Many People Will See Your Speaker?

**Under 50 people:**
- Small meeting room, intimate event, or boardroom setting
- ✅ Recommended: Tablet or small monitor system
- Why: Audience is close; small display is sufficient
- Cost: $300-600 per event

**50-200 people:**
- Medium conference room, small ballroom, or local event
- ✅ Recommended: Professional monitor or tablet system
- Why: Display needs to be visible from 15-25 feet
- Cost: $600-1,200 per event

**200-500 people:**
- Hotel ballroom, convention center, or mid-size venue
- ✅ Recommended: Professional monitor system (15-24" display)
- Why: Larger display readable from 20-40 feet
- Cost: $1,200-1,800 per event

**500+ people:**
- Large venue, stadium, or outdoor event
- ✅ Recommended: Large professional monitors OR glass teleprompter
- Why: Distance and room size require larger displays or invisible glass system
- Cost: $1,500-3,500+ per event

---

### Question 2: What's Your Budget?

**Budget: Under $600**
- Best option: Consumer iPad/tablet system or small monitor
- Setup: DIY or basic professional setup
- Limitation: Limited visibility distance, basic operator
- Use case: Very small meetings, internal presentations, webinars

**Budget: $600-1,200**
- Best option: Professional monitor system
- Setup: Professional-grade equipment, experienced operator
- Includes: Equipment, operator, basic setup (1-2 hours)
- Use case: Corporate events, trade shows, local events

**Budget: $1,200-2,000**
- Best option: Professional monitor system with full day support
- Setup: Professional equipment, experienced operator, rehearsal time
- Includes: Equipment, operator, setup, rehearsal (4-6 hours), backup system
- Use case: Important corporate events, earnings calls, major presentations

**Budget: $2,000-3,500**
- Best option: Glass teleprompter OR professional monitor system with full production support
- Setup: Premium equipment, specialized operator, full day availability
- Includes: Equipment, experienced operator, full rehearsal, technical director
- Use case: High-profile events, formal occasions, video production integration

**Budget: $3,500+**
- Best option: Custom integrated system, dual operators, full AV production
- Setup: Premium everything
- Includes: Multiple systems, backup operators, video production coordination
- Use case: Major corporate events, shareholder meetings, broadcast-quality productions

### Question 3: What Type of Event Is This?

**Corporate/Business Event**
- Town hall, earnings call, board presentation, investor meeting
- ✅ Best choice: Professional monitor system or on-camera system
- Why: Executive presence matters; transparency expected; budget available
- Typical cost: $1,200-2,500

**Live Public Event**
- Political rally, trade convention, award show, sports event
- ✅ Best choice: Professional monitor system or glass teleprompter
- Why: Large audience, need invisible system or professional appearance
- Typical cost: $1,200-3,500

**Video Production**
- Commercial, corporate video, recorded message, social content
- ✅ Best choice: Professional on-camera system
- Why: Optimization for camera rather than in-person audience
- Typical cost: $1,000-2,000 per production day

**Religious/Nonprofit Event**
- Church service, nonprofit fundraiser, community event
- ✅ Best choice: Monitor system or glass teleprompter (budget-dependent)
- Why: Professional appearance appreciated; budget often limited
- Typical cost: $800-1,500

**Political Event**
- Campaign rally, town hall, press conference
- ✅ Best choice: Glass teleprompter (professional/invisible requirement)
- Why: Professional appearance essential; large outdoor audiences often
- Typical cost: $2,000-4,000+

**Small Internal Meeting**
- Team presentation, board meeting, small seminar
- ✅ Best choice: Tablet system or basic monitor
- Why: Intimate setting; simpler equipment sufficient
- Typical cost: $300-800

---

### Question 4: What's Your Speaker's Experience Level?

**Experienced Speaker** (performed many times, comfortable with public speaking)
- ✅ Can handle: Any system
- Recommendation: Go with glass teleprompter if budget allows (more professional appearance)
- Rehearsal needed: 20-30 minutes minimum
- Additional cost: Minimal

**Moderate Experience** (has spoken before, somewhat comfortable)
- ✅ Best choice: Professional monitor system
- Recommendation: Good visibility without intimidating equipment
- Rehearsal needed: 30-60 minutes recommended
- Additional cost: $200-400 for rehearsal time

**Novice Speaker** (first time, nervous, unprepared)
- ✅ Best choice: Professional monitor system with rehearsal
- Recommendation: Familiar equipment, experienced operator, rehearsal critical
- Rehearsal needed: 1-2 hours minimum
- Additional cost: $300-600 for extended rehearsal
- Note: Consider professional coaching ($500-1,000) if very high stakes

**Multiple Speakers** (4+ people speaking in sequence)
- ✅ Best choice: Professional system with experienced operator
- Recommendation: Operator manages script transitions; consistent setup for all speakers
- Rehearsal needed: Each speaker gets 15-20 minutes individually
- Additional cost: $200-500 for multi-speaker coordination

---

### Question 5: What's Your Venue Like?

**Indoor Theater/Ballroom**
- ✅ Best choice: Glass teleprompter OR professional monitor
- Equipment visibility: Excellent; climate controlled
- Recommendation: Glass if budget allows (most professional look)
- Typical cost: $1,500-3,000

**Hotel Ballroom/Conference Room**
- ✅ Best choice: Professional monitor system
- Equipment visibility: Good; standard lighting
- Recommendation: Monitor system balances cost and professionalism
- Typical cost: $1,000-2,000

**Corporate Boardroom**
- ✅ Best choice: Tablet or small monitor system
- Equipment visibility: Close distances
- Recommendation: Minimal/hidden system appropriate
- Typical cost: $400-800

**Outdoor Venue**
- ✅ Best choice: Professional bright monitor system
- Equipment visibility: Need high brightness; weatherproofing critical
- Recommendation: Monitor system with outdoor optimization
- Typical cost: $1,500-2,500

**Stadium/Large Outdoor**
- ✅ Best choice: Glass teleprompter system
- Equipment visibility: Invisible to large audiences
- Recommendation: Necessary for large-scale outdoor events
- Typical cost: $2,500-4,000+

**Virtual/Webinar**
- ✅ Best choice: Tablet system positioned next to camera
- Equipment visibility: Only camera sees setup
- Recommendation: Simple, professional-looking setup
- Typical cost: $400-800

**Multiple Venues** (traveling event)
- ✅ Best choice: Portable monitor system
- Equipment visibility: Flexible; easy to move
- Recommendation: Q-Gear or Prompter People systems (road-tested)
- Typical cost: $1,200-2,000

## Common Mistakes in Choosing Teleprompters

### ❌ Mistake 1: Choosing DIY Equipment for Professional Event
**Problem:** Consumer-grade equipment looks unprofessional; operator lacks experience; failure means disaster
**Solution:** Invest in professional equipment and operator for any event with 50+ attendees

### ❌ Mistake 2: Skipping Rehearsal to Save Money
**Problem:** Speaker doesn't practice with equipment; stumbles during event; operator doesn't know speaker's pace
**Solution:** Budget 30-60 minutes minimum rehearsal time. It's worth it.

### ❌ Mistake 3: Choosing Glass Teleprompter When Not Needed
**Problem:** Unnecessary expense; more complex setup; overkill for audience size
**Solution:** Monitor system is sufficient for most events. Glass only needed for 500+ or formal occasions.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Ignoring Operator Experience
**Problem:** Cheap operator scrolls at fixed pace; doesn't adapt to speaker; looks jerky and distracting
**Solution:** Professional operator with broadcast/event experience is non-negotiable.

### ❌ Mistake 5: Not Communicating Event Details
**Problem:** Wrong equipment arrives; setup doesn't work for venue; operator not prepared
**Solution:** Provide clear details: venue size, audience, speaker info, lighting, technical setup

### ❌ Mistake 6: Assuming Teleprompter Will Fix Poor Speech
**Problem:** Badly-written speech still sounds bad, just read more smoothly
**Solution:** Good teleprompter supports good speech; it's not a replacement for good content

## Questions to Ask Your Teleprompter Provider

When you contact a teleprompter service, ask these questions:

1. **Equipment Options:** What systems do you offer? What's recommended for my audience size?
2. **Operator Experience:** How many years of experience? What kind of events? References?
3. **Rehearsal Options:** How much rehearsal time is included? What's the cost of additional rehearsal?
4. **Backup Systems:** What happens if equipment fails? Do you have backup systems?
5. **Venue Coordination:** Can you work with my venue's AV team?
6. **Setup Time:** How long do you need for setup? What time should we coordinate arrival?
7. **Pricing Breakdown:** What's included in the quoted price? What costs extra?
8. **Last-Minute Changes:** Can script be modified on the fly if needed?
9. **Technical Support:** Who handles troubleshooting during event?
10. **Insurance/Liability:** Are you insured? What's your cancellation policy?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I rent a teleprompter system without hiring an operator?**
A: Technically yes, but not recommended. Professional operators make the difference between natural delivery and jerky, robotic reading.

**Q: What if my speaker is very nervous?**
A: Budget for extra rehearsal time ($200-400 additional). Practice builds confidence.

**Q: Can I use the same system for multiple events?**
A: Yes. If you have recurring events, discuss renting regularly for better rates.

**Q: What's the difference between "professional" and "consumer" teleprompter systems?**
A: Professional systems are broadcast-grade, more durable, more visible from distance, and operators are trained to use them. Consumer systems are less reliable and look less professional.

**Q: Is glass teleprompter really invisible?**
A: Yes, to the audience. The specially treated glass reflects the image only from below and in front. It's invisible to cameras and audiences beyond 15 feet.
