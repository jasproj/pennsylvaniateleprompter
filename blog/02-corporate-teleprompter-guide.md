## Why Corporate Leaders Use Teleprompters

### Myth vs. Reality

**Myth:** "Using a teleprompter makes me look less authentic."
**Reality:** When you're reading from a teleprompter properly, most people can't tell. What they see is a confident executive who knows exactly what they want to say.

**Myth:** "I should be able to deliver my speech without notes."
**Reality:** Executives juggle hundreds of priorities. Professional speakers (politicians, celebrities, CEOs) use teleprompters because they enable polish without the stress of memorization.

**Myth:** "Teleprompters are just for big events."
**Reality:** Corporate teams use teleprompters for internal meetings, webinars, recorded messages, and board presentations regularly.

### Real Benefits of Corporate Teleprompters

✅ **Message Control:** Exact wording ensures compliance with investor relations guidelines, legal approval, or marketing messaging.

✅ **Consistency:** Multiple executives delivering the same message? Teleprompters ensure alignment.

✅ **Professional Appearance:** Confidence shows. Executives who trust their equipment deliver stronger presence.

✅ **Time Efficiency:** Executives can focus on delivery, not memorization. Less rehearsal stress.

✅ **Flexibility:** Last-minute changes are possible without disrupting delivery.

✅ **Audience Confidence:** Investors, employees, and media interpret polished delivery as organizational stability.

## How Corporate Teleprompters Work in Different Settings

### In-Person Event (Town Hall, Earnings Call, Board Meeting)

**Setup:**
1. Displays positioned on either side of stage or podium
2. Executive sees 24-point font from 8-12 feet away
3. Operator positioned offstage with wireless tablet control
4. Backup system ready if primary fails
5. Audio/video feeds integrated with event AV

**Delivery:**
- Executive appears to address the room (teleprompter is peripheral vision)
- Operator adjusts scroll based on executive's pace
- Executive can ad-lib or deviate from script—operator follows
- Event continues smoothly even if minor technical issues occur

### Recorded Corporate Video

**Setup:**
1. Executive seated or standing in studio/office
2. Camera positioned at eye level (teleprompter below camera)
3. Professional lighting (key light, fill, back light)
4. Operator monitors video feed and controls scroll
5. Multiple takes possible for perfection

**Delivery:**
- Executive delivers to camera as if speaking to individual viewer
- Intimate, direct communication style
- Operator stops between takes for adjustments
- Final product looks natural and confident

### Virtual/Webinar Presentation

**Setup:**
1. Executive at computer/desk
2. Teleprompter tablet positioned next to camera
3. Lighting optimized for video capture
4. Operator manages scroll from control tablet
5. Minimal visible equipment

**Delivery:**
- Executive speaks directly into camera
- Camera captures what Zoom audience sees
- Operator ensures smooth, professional pace
- Chat/Q&A handled by separate person or during breaks

## Best Practices for Corporate Teleprompter Use

### Script Preparation

**Format Matters:**
- Large font (28-32pt for executives, varies by distance)
- Corporate-style formatting (Arial, Helvetica, Calibri)
- Single or 1.5 spacing
- Generous margins for operator notes
- Key points highlighted or in caps

**Content Standards:**
- Executive-level language (clear, authoritative, not casual)
- Exactly timed script (test read aloud first)
- Smooth transitions between sections
- Emphasis marks for tone shifts
- Talking points formatted separately from full speech

### Rehearsal Protocol

**Minimum Rehearsal:**
- Simple messages: 20-30 minutes with operator
- Important announcements: 1-2 hours
- Critical events (earnings, major announcements): 2-4 hours full run-through

**What Happens in Rehearsal:**
1. Executive reads through script once (operator times it)
2. Operator adjusts font size/scroll based on natural pace
3. Executive delivers 2-3 full run-throughs at performance pace
4. Operator notes timing variations and adjusts
5. Final dress rehearsal with all AV elements if applicable

### Day-of Protocol

- 30-45 minutes before event: Arrive for setup and tech checks
- 15-20 minutes before: Quick final run-through
- 5 minutes before: Final equipment checks
- Go signal from operator when ready

## Corporate Teleprompter FAQ

**Q: Will investors/employees think I'm reading if I use a teleprompter?**
A: No—if it's done professionally. Executive speakers at Fortune 500 companies use teleprompters routinely. The key is rehearsal so your delivery is natural, not mechanical.

**Q: Can I deviate from the script?**
A: Yes. Experienced operators can follow your ad-libs and get back on script seamlessly.

**Q: What if I make a mistake?**
A: Keep going naturally. Operator keeps pace. Most audiences won't notice.

**Q: What if we need to change the script last-minute?**
A: Professional operators can load new scripts or edit on the fly in most cases. Always notify ASAP so operator has time to prepare.

**Q: How do you handle multiple executives speaking in sequence?**
A: Operator loads each script separately. We practice transitions between speakers. Setup is quick between speakers.

**Q: Will the teleprompter show up on video if we're recording?**
A: No. Professional on-camera systems position teleprompter below or to the side of camera—it's invisible in the final video.

**Q: What about virtual events (Zoom, Teams, etc.)?**
A: Works perfectly. Teleprompter positioned next to camera. Operator manages scroll. Audience only sees executive on camera—no equipment visible.
